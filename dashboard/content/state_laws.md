## Conclusion: seven months until the federal landscape transforms

This report captures a regulatory environment in unprecedented flux. The **November 12, 2026 federal deadline** under P.L. 119-37 represents the single most consequential event ahead — the 0.4 mg per-container cap and total-THC standard will eliminate the vast majority of hemp cannabinoid products from legal commerce unless Congress acts to delay or modify Section 781. The Hemp Planting Predictability Act and CSRA both remain stalled without committee hearings.

For **kratom operators**, the landscape is fragmenting rapidly. Kansas became the 9th ban state just yesterday. California's administrative enforcement has proven devastatingly effective without new legislation. The FDA's 7-OH scheduling recommendation to DEA remains pending — when DEA acts, it will trigger a national compliance event that distinguishes whole-leaf kratom from concentrated/synthetic 7-OH products. States with KCPA frameworks (now ~18–20) offer the most predictable operating environment.

For **7-OH specifically**, Ohio's emergency rule (expiring ~June 10, 2026) is the only action specifically naming MGM-15. Federal scheduling of 7-OH appears likely within 12–18 months based on congressional pressure and the FDA recommendation. Operators should plan for this eventuality.

For **functional mushroom products**, the regulatory environment remains uniformly favorable — legal in all 50 states under standard dietary supplement frameworks. This is the lowest-risk product category in the portfolio.

For **psilocybin**, the therapeutic access landscape continues expanding (Oregon operational, Colorado licensing, New Mexico medical program, multiple state research pilots), but commercial distribution remains impossible under current federal Schedule I classification. The Compass Pathways NDA (expected late 2026–2027) could catalyze federal rescheduling.

**Three strategic imperatives for operators:**
1. Prepare contingency plans for the November 2026 federal hemp deadline — diversify toward non-THC cannabinoids or pursue licensed cannabis channels in applicable states
2. Separate whole-leaf kratom operations from concentrated 7-OH products immediately, as regulatory treatment increasingly diverges
3. Prioritize distribution in KCPA states and favorable hemp markets (IL, MO, NC, PA, OK, NE) while avoiding or exiting high-risk markets (CA, CT, OH, VA)
