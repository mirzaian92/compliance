## Federal baseline refresh

**P.L. 119-37** (signed November 12, 2025; effective November 12, 2026) rewrites the federal hemp definition to measure **total THC** — including THCa and all isomers — rather than delta-9 alone. Finished hemp-derived cannabinoid products exceeding **0.4 mg total THC per container** are excluded from the hemp definition and fall under the Controlled Substances Act. Synthetically derived cannabinoids are also excluded. Legislative efforts to delay the effective date (H.R. 7024/S. 3686, pushing to 2028) and to repeal outright (H.R. 6209) remain pending but have not advanced. Until November 12, 2026, the 2018 Farm Bill's delta-9-only standard at ≤0.3% dry weight remains operative federally.

On **July 29, 2025**, the FDA formally recommended that the DEA classify **7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH)** as a Schedule I controlled substance, targeting concentrated and synthetic 7-OH products while explicitly excluding natural kratom leaf. The DEA has **not initiated rulemaking** as of April 2026 — 7-OH remains federally unscheduled, though congressional pressure is mounting (Rep. Bresnahan letter March 27, 2026; Sen. Marshall letter urging emergency scheduling of 7-OH, MGM-15, and mitragynine pseudoindoxyl). Whole-leaf kratom remains federally unscheduled. Psilocybin remains Schedule I federally, with DEA research quotas rising to 50,000 grams for 2026. Functional mushrooms (lion's mane, reishi, chaga, cordyceps, turkey tail) remain legal dietary supplements under DSHEA. **MGM-15** (mitragynine pseudoindoxyl / dihydro-7-hydroxy mitragynine) is not federally scheduled; only Ohio has specifically named it in a scheduling action (Board of Pharmacy emergency rule, December 12, 2025).
